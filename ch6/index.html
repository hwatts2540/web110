Planets

Our solar system is home to eight fascinating planets, each with unique characteristics, from the scorching surface of Mercury to the icy depths of Neptune. Beyond our solar system, exoplanets orbit distant stars, some of which may even harbor life.

Learn more about planets: NASA Solar System Exploration

Stars

Stars are luminous spheres of plasma held together by their own gravity. They are the fundamental building blocks of galaxies, producing light and heat through nuclear fusion. From our own Sun to distant supergiants, stars come in a dazzling array of sizes and colors.

Discover more about stars: National Geographic - Stars

Galaxies

Galaxies are vast systems of stars, stellar remnants, interstellar gas, dust, and dark matter, all bound together by gravity. They come in various shapes, including spiral, elliptical, and irregular, each housing billions to trillions of stars. Our own galaxy is the Milky Way.

Explore galaxies: Britannica - Galaxy
